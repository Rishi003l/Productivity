Effective Techniques

Pomodoro Technique

The Pomodoro Technique is a time management method developed by Francesco Cirillo. Work in focused 25-minute intervals, followed by 5-minute breaks.

How it works:

  1. Choose a task to work on
  2. Set the timer for 25 minutes
  3. Work on the task until the timer rings
  4. Take a short 5-minute break
  5. After 4 pomodoros, take a longer 15-30 minute break

Feynman Technique

Named after physicist Richard Feynman, this technique helps you understand concepts by explaining them in simple terms as if teaching someone else.

The four steps:

  1. Choose a concept to learn
  2. Explain it to someone (or pretend to) using simple language
  3. Identify gaps in your explanation and revisit sources
  4. Simplify and use analogies to strengthen understanding

Spaced Repetition

A learning technique that incorporates increasing intervals of time between reviews of previously learned material to exploit the psychological spacing effect.

Implementation:

  1. Learn new information
  2. Review after 1 day
  3. Review after 3 days
  4. Review after 7 days
  5. Review after 14 days, then monthly
Plan Your Reviews

Active Recall

Instead of passively consuming information, actively stimulate your memory by testing yourself. This builds stronger neural pathways.

Practical tips:

  • Create flashcards for key concepts
  • Write questions at the end of your notes
  • Close your book and recite what you've learned
  • Teach the material to someone else
  • Use practice exams and quizzes

Pareto Principle (80/20 Rule)

80% of results come from 20% of efforts. Identify and focus on the most important tasks or concepts for maximum effectiveness.

Application:

  • Identify the core 20% of material that covers 80% of what you need to know
  • Focus on the most important tasks first
  • Eliminate or delegate low-impact activities
  • Regularly audit your time and activities
Prioritize Your Tasks

Mind Mapping

Visual thinking tool that helps structure information in a way that mirrors how our brains actually work - with radiating ideas connected to a central concept.

Creating effective mind maps:

  1. Start with a central idea in the middle
  2. Use colors, images and symbols
  3. Connect main branches to the central idea
  4. Use one keyword per line
  5. Develop sub-branches for related concepts
